Merrick is earnestly pursuing the mission to empower anyone to write software without code. This isn't an abstract interest — it's a conviction rooted in economic equality. When the ability to build software is locked behind years of specialized training, it concentrates power and opportunity in the hands of a few. Merrick wants to break that open.

This is why he's spent years at Webflow building the tools that let people create for the web without writing code — and why he's drawn to companies working on the same frontier.

## What He Cares About

**Technical Quality**: Merrick doesn't cut corners. He builds constraint engines, type systems, and programming languages because he believes the quality of the foundation determines the quality of everything built on top of it. He's emotionally invested in craft — not as vanity, but because people depend on what he ships.

**Mission-Oriented Companies**: Merrick is looking for companies where the mission is real, not decorative. He wants to work somewhere the mission drives decisions, not just the pitch deck. He's at his best when the work matters beyond the quarterly numbers.

## What This Means for You

If your company is working to make software creation accessible to more people, if you care about technical excellence, and if your mission is something you'd pursue even when it's hard — Merrick wants to talk.
